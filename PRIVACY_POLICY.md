## 2. Information We Collect

CyberByte is designed with a **privacy-first architecture**. We do not require registration, login, or any form of user account.

### 2.1 Information Stored Locally on Your Device

The App stores the following data **exclusively on your device** using local storage (AsyncStorage). This data is never transmitted to us or to any third party:

| Data | Purpose | Storage |
|------|---------|---------|
| Module completion status | To track which learning modules you have completed | Device only (AsyncStorage) |
| Device theme preference | To automatically configure Light or Dark mode | Read-only system setting |
| Device language | To automatically configure the App's display language (EN, PT, FR, DE) | Read-only system setting |

### 2.2 Information We Do NOT Collect

We want to be explicit: CyberByte does **not** collect, process, store, or transmit any of the following:

- Personal identifiers (name, email, phone number, address)
- Account or authentication credentials
- Location data (GPS or network-based)
- Contacts, calendar, or call logs
- Camera, microphone, or sensor data
- Advertising identifiers (IDFA / GAID)
- Browsing or search history
- Financial or payment information
- Biometric data
- Device identifiers beyond what is described in Section 2.3

### 2.3 Information Processed by Third-Party Services

The App's **Cyber News** feature fetches live RSS feeds through the **rss2json.com** API. When this feature is used, the following may be processed by the third-party service:

| Data | Recipient | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| IP address | rss2json.com / Cloudflare | Standard server-client communication |
| User-Agent string | rss2json.com / Cloudflare | Standard HTTP request header |
| Requested feed URL | rss2json.com | To convert the RSS feed to JSON format |

This data is transmitted as part of standard HTTP requests and is not initiated, stored, or controlled by us. The rss2json.com service operates behind **Cloudflare** infrastructure, which may process temporary telemetry data (IP, request headers) for security and rate-limiting purposes.

We do not have access to any data processed by rss2json.com or Cloudflare.

## 6. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you have the following rights regarding your data:

### 6.1 Under the GDPR (EU/EEA)

- **Right of access** (Art. 15) — Request confirmation of what data is processed
- **Right to rectification** (Art. 16) — Request correction of inaccurate data
- **Right to erasure** (Art. 17) — Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
- **Right to restriction** (Art. 18) — Request limitation of processing
- **Right to data portability** (Art. 20) — Receive your data in a structured format
- **Right to object** (Art. 21) — Object to processing based on legitimate interest
- **Right to lodge a complaint** — You may file a complaint with your national Data Protection Authority. In Portugal, this is the **Comissão Nacional de Proteção de Dados (CNPD)** — [www.cnpd.pt](https://www.cnpd.pt)

### 6.2 Under the LGPD (Brazil)

You have equivalent rights under the LGPD, including access, correction, anonymization, deletion, and information about third-party sharing. You may contact the **Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados (ANPD)** — [www.gov.br/anpd](https://www.gov.br/anpd).

### 6.3 Under the CCPA (California, USA)

- **Right to know** — What personal information is collected, used, or shared
- **Right to delete** — Request deletion of personal information
- **Right to opt-out of sale** — We do not sell personal information
- **Right to non-discrimination** — Exercise of rights will not affect your use of the App

Since CyberByte does not collect personal information, these rights are inherently satisfied by the App's privacy-first design. If you have questions or wish to exercise any right, please contact us at the email below.
